LMSS V3 Guide

Everything you need to participate in the V3 SALI taxonomy — from browsing the standard to contributing through working groups.

Open V3 LMSS Viewer
1

Via the V3 LMSS Viewer

The V3 LMSS Viewer is the public home of the V3 SALI taxonomy — a browsable, searchable interface where anyone can explore the standard, follow changes, and comment on proposals during open consultation periods. (LMSS V2 remains available at viewer.sali.org.)

2

Via Working Groups

Working groups are the development backbone of LMSS. Open to SALI users, they meet regularly to review proposals, deliberate on contested nodes, and decide what enters each stable release.

Part 1 The V3 LMSS Viewer
Access

Who can do what

The V3 LMSS Viewer is open to everyone. Deeper participation — posting comments, editing nodes — is available through a simple request or working group appointment.

Level What you can do How to get it
Reader Browse the full taxonomy, view node definitions and properties, search, download exports, and view the comments feed. No login required. Available to anyone — just open v3.sali.org. (LMSS V2 remains at viewer.sali.org.)
Commentator Everything above, plus post comments on nodes during an RFC (Request for Comment) period. Email info@sali.org to request access. Open to any SALI member or interested practitioner.
Editor Everything above, plus create and edit nodes, propose withdrawals, and make structural changes. All edits are tracked with the editor's identity. Appointed by a SALI working group.

The vast majority of users will interact with the LMSS Viewer as Readers — no account needed, no barriers. Commentator access is there if you want to engage directly in a consultation period.

Beta

A new node enters the taxonomy in Beta status — visible, searchable, and available for use. Beta indicates the node is a live proposal, not a final commitment. The majority of new V3 additions begin here.

RFC

For significant proposals, the working group opens a formal Request for Comment period — typically 60 days. A banner appears in the LMSS Viewer and commentators can post responses directly on the relevant nodes.

Accepted

After review, the working group accepts the node: the Beta label is removed and it enters the next annual stable release. Accepted nodes are the appropriate reference for production integrations.

Withdrawn

Nodes the working group decides not to accept are withdrawn — removed from the viewer and search. They are retained in an audit log and can be reinstated if circumstances change.

Participation

Commenting during an RFC

When a Request for Comment period is open, the LMSS Viewer signals this prominently and invites structured community input on the nodes under review.

A banner appears across the top of the V3 LMSS Viewer showing the RFC title, a brief description of what is under review, and the comment deadline. Nodes in scope show a comment section directly in their detail panel.

How to participate

  • Log in with a Commentator account — or email info@sali.org to request one.
  • Navigate to any node within the scope of the RFC.
  • Use the Add Comment function to post your input, selecting whether it applies to the label, definition, parent classification, or the node generally.
  • Comments are immediately visible to all users and to the working group.

You do not need to attend a working group meeting for your comment to be considered. The group reads all submissions before reaching a decision.

No account? You can still read all existing comments on any node without logging in. Only posting requires a Commentator account.

Discovery

The Comments Feed

The Comments Feed surfaces all discussions across the taxonomy in one place — useful for following active consultations without navigating node by node.

The Comments button in the LMSS Viewer toolbar opens the Comments Feed — a reverse-chronological list of all comments across all nodes, with a direct link from each entry to the relevant node.

The feed is visible to everyone without login. Comments posted since your last visit are highlighted so you can quickly spot new activity.

For working group participants and editors, the feed is the fastest way to stay across community input during an active RFC period without visiting every affected node individually.

Releases

Stable releases and the edit log

The LMSS Viewer tracks two states: the live Beta branch, updated continuously, and annual stable releases for production use.

Annual stable releases

Stable releases are point-in-time snapshots of all accepted nodes, published in Excel, JSON, and CSV. These are the appropriate reference for production integrations that need a fixed, versioned target.

The live taxonomy

The LMSS Viewer always reflects the current Beta branch — updated in real time as editors make changes and nodes are promoted. Vendors and firms who need access to new nodes before the next release can track this directly.

Edit Log and V2 → V3 Log

The Edit Log in the toolbar shows a running record of all changes: nodes added, labels updated, parents moved, nodes withdrawn, and definitions edited — with timestamps and the identity of the editor who made each change.

The V2 → V3 Log is a downloadable Excel report documenting every change between LMSS Version 2 and the current Version 3, useful for firms migrating from V2.

Part 2 Working Groups
Governance

Contributing through working groups

Working groups are the primary mechanism through which the taxonomy evolves. They meet regularly to review proposals, manage RFC periods, and decide what enters each stable release.

Working group participation is open to SALI members. Members with sufficient domain expertise may be appointed as Editors, giving them the ability to propose and edit nodes in the LMSS Viewer directly.

To join a working group, contact info@sali.org or reach out directly to the working group chair.
